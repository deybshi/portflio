My Personal Blog.

Welcome to my website. Here I share stories about programming, networking, friendships, and the also the course itself.

Programming

In my first year, I was introduced to basic programming. At first, coding was very confusing. I had a hard time understanding errors in my program. Sometimes I felt frustrated because even a small mistake, like a missing semicolon, could stop my whole code from running. But slowly, I learned how to read errors and fix them. I realized that programming is like solving a puzzle. It needs patience, focus, and practice. The feeling of seeing my program run successfully and also impressed ma’am Vera, made me proud and motivated me to learn more.

Networking

Another subject that challenged me was networking. I learned about IP addresses, routers, switches, and different network models. At first, the concepts were difficult to understand because they were new to me. But when our teacher explained real-life examples, I started to understand how the internet works. I also enjoyed learning about cybersecurity because it taught me how to protect information and stay safe online. It made me more aware of the risks in the digital world.

Struggles at my course

Being an IT student is not always easy because it comes with many struggles and challenges. There are times when the lessons become difficult, the code does not work, and understanding complex topics takes a lot of time and effort. Many nights are spent studying, practicing, and trying to solve problems that seem impossible at first. Despite these struggles, the experience teaches patience, perseverance, and the importance of never giving up while continuing to learn and improve in the field of technology.

Programming ulit hehe

Being an IT student is exciting because of creating projects like simple websites and working with teammates. Although there are disagreements, teamwork helps improve technical, communication, and leadership skills. The journey is challenging with sleepless nights, debugging, and moments of self-doubt, but these challenges help me grow and learn. Studying IT also helped me understand the importance and impact of technology in everyday life.

Friends

Studying IT has also helped me build many friendships along the way. In our classes, we often work together on coding projects, help each other understand difficult lessons, and share ideas about technology. Through these experiences, I was able to meet many people who have the same interests in programming and computers. These friendships make the journey in IT more enjoyable, because we support and motivate each other to keep learning and improving.

BASTA CICT, QUALITY!

As I continue my journey as an IT student, I know there is still a lot to learn. Technology is always changing, and I must keep improving my skills. Even though the path is not easy, I am proud of the progress I have made. Being an Information Technology student is not just about learning computers. It is about solving problems, thinking logically, and preparing for a future in a digital world.
